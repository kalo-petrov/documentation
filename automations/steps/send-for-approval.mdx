## What it does

The **Send for Approval** step works identically to the Send Email step, except the email is **not sent immediately**. Instead, it's queued for manual review. An approver (you or a teammate) reviews the drafted email and either approves it (which sends it) or rejects it (which discards it).

---

## When to use it

- Invoices or payment emails that a finance manager should review before delivery
- Partner communications that require sign-off from a manager
- New affiliates where you want to personally check the first automated email
- Any situation where "set it and forget it" feels too risky

---

## How Approval Works

1. The automation runs and builds the draft email (with all variables substituted)
2. Instead of sending, the email is placed in the **Approval Queue**
3. You (or a designated approver) receives a notification
4. The approver goes to the approval page, reads the drafted email, and clicks **Approve** or **Reject**
5. Approved emails are sent immediately; rejected ones are discarded with a note

---

## Configuration

Configuration is identical to the [Send Email step](/automations/steps/send-email):

- **Send From** — which email account to use for sending (after approval)
- **Recipients** — who the email goes to once approved
- **Subject** — supports variables
- **Body** — full HTML editor with variable insertion
- **Template** — optionally load from a saved email template
- **Invoice Attachment** — optionally attach a generated invoice PDF

---

## Reviewing Pending Approvals

Pending approvals appear in the **Runs & Logs** section, under the specific automation run. Click the pending item to:

- Preview the full email (subject, body, recipients, any attachments)
- Click **Approve** to send immediately
- Click **Reject** to discard and leave a rejection reason

<Note>
  Approved emails use the same email account and recipients configured in the step — no changes can be made at approval time. If you want to modify the content before sending, reject it, edit the automation, and run again.
</Note>

---

## Expiry

Pending approvals don't expire automatically. They remain in the queue until approved or rejected. However, if the underlying data is time-sensitive (e.g. "last week's stats"), delay in approval means the email's content may be outdated by the time it's sent.

<Tip>
  For time-sensitive data, approve emails promptly or switch to the regular Send Email step with manual monitoring via Runs & Logs.
</Tip>
